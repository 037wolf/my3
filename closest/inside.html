History

The Nissan GT-R R35, often simply referred to as the GT-R, is an iconic sports car that has captivated automotive enthusiasts worldwide. Here are some fascinating facts about this legendary machine:

The GT-R R35, launched in 2007, is the successor to a long line of famous Nissan Skyline GT-R models. It represents the fifth generation of the GT-R lineage and marked a significant departure from its predecessors by adopting a standalone "GT-R" branding.

Nissan has equipped the GT-R R35 with cutting-edge technology to enhance its handling, stability, and driver experience. Features like the ATTESA E-TS all-wheel-drive system, the adjustable Bilstein DampTronic suspension, and the sophisticated Vehicle Dynamic Control (VDC) system all contribute to its impressive performance.

The GT-R R35 boasts a distinctive and aggressive design, characterized by its sharp lines and signature quad-circle taillights. This design not only enhances aerodynamics but also contributes to the car's unmistakable presence on the road.
